The Box That
Lifts Its Own Lid

This week's design brief: design a finger-jointed storage box using laser cutting. What started as a simple cardboard template became a gear-lifted lid with an internally lit, perforated pattern — six attempts in the making.

Concept: A storage box where two interlocking gears raise the lid when turned, with a lattice-cut pattern on the sides that lets light bleed out from the inside, creating a visually engaging and functional piece. It is a box that aesthetically blends form and function.

Final Result

The completed box: a gear-lifted lid, a perforated side pattern, and LEDs wired along the inner border, controlled by an Arduino Uno R3 following a tutorial from RandomNerdTutorials.

Final box — gear-lifted lid, lit from inside

Gear mechanism lifting the lid, LEDs lit via Arduino Uno R3

Electronics

LEDs were placed along the interior border of the box, wired to an Arduino Uno R3 following the RandomNerdTutorials guide for basic LED control. The light filters out through the perforated lattice pattern on the sides, lighting up the cuts and creating a visually engaging effect.

Process - Six Iterations

Every iteration taught something. Here's the honest account of what worked, what didn't, and what changed each time.

Research & Inspiration

My process began with two moodboards guiding the direction - one exploring woodworking joinery and craft precedents, the other mapping out specific laser-cutting techniques to experiment with.

Left: joinery and craft references · Right: laser cut technique explorations

Attempt 01

Too small

The Template Test

I started with the base template straight from the assignment. I cut it to test finger joints, but the scale was completely wrong for a shoebox-size storage piece. It needed to be significantly scaled up before anything else could be explored.

Attempt 02

Concept not resolved

The Flexible Lid

I scaled up the box and tried a perforated flexible lid in cardboard. The flexibility worked mechanically but felt underwhelming in cardboard — not exciting enough as a material. The concept needed to push further.

Attempt 03

Concept unlocked

Cardboard Gears

First full concept test — pattern cuts on the sides, cardboard gear mechanism for the lid. The pattern looked promising, but cardboard gears could not handle the weight of the lid at full scale.

Attempt 04

Material upgrade

Plywood Gears

Fleshed-out pattern on the sides, switched to plywood gears for the mechanism. Stronger, but the rest of the box was still cardboard — a mismatch that needed addressing.

Attempt 05

Structural failure

Press-Fit, Plywood Lid

Tried making the whole box press-fit with a plywood lid. The lid was too heavy for the cardboard body to support - it broke under the weight.

Attempt 06

Final build

Plywood + LEDs

Dialed back to a cardboard body to comply with the assignment, but kept plywood gears and changed their placement. With the gear mechanism holding properly, I wired an LED strip along the interior border via an Arduino Uno R3. Light filters through the perforated pattern achieving the desired effect.
